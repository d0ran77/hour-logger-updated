Privacy & Terms

Last Updated: March 2026

1. Privacy Policy & Google API Usage

Practisy is designed as a "Local-First" application. This means your data privacy is absolute by design.

How we use Google User Data

Practisy requests read-only access to your Google Calendar (https://www.googleapis.com/auth/calendar.readonly). We use this access exclusively to search for calendar events matching the keywords you specify (e.g., "Client", "Supervision") in order to automate your clinical logging.

Data Storage and Sharing

We do not have a database. All data retrieved from your Google Calendar, alongside your clinical notes and settings, is stored locally in your device's browser (via LocalStorage). We do not transmit, store, or share your Google Calendar data, clinical logs, or personal information with any external servers, third parties, or analytics providers.

Google API Services User Data Policy Compliance

Practisy's use and transfer to any other app of information received from Google APIs will adhere to the Google API Services User Data Policy, including the Limited Use requirements.

2. Terms of Service

By using Practisy, you agree to the following terms:

  • Data Responsibility: Because Practisy is entirely local to your device, you are solely responsible for maintaining backups of your data. We cannot recover lost, deleted, or corrupted data.
  • Professional Ethics: Practisy provides tracking utilities "as is" without warranty. You remain solely responsible for your professional ethics and compliance with your respective governing bodies (e.g., BACP, UKCP, APA).
  • Anonymity: We strongly advise using pseudonyms or initials (e.g., 'Client A') in your calendar events and clinical notes to maintain absolute client anonymity.

If you wish to remove your data from Practisy, you may use the "Erase All Local Data" button within the application, or clear your browser's site data.
